Collect data on areas greater than 90 by 90 meters. If you are unable to find a pure area of 90 by 90 meters and a second crop is present, please document it using the secondary crop field.
If you are not at the center of the area, capture the bearing and distance to the center of the field.
Try to space your data collection by over 1 km unless nearby sample units are of different classes.
Two or three photos for each location with one covering the entire field and another with a close-up of the crop. Please take your photos in landscape view from where you recorded the position.
Cropland is defined as all cultivated plants harvested for food, feed, and fiber, including plantations (e.g., orchards, vineyards, coffee, tea, rubber).
Crop Intensity is the number of cropping cycles in a 12 month period.
Irrigation is defined as artificial application of any amount of water to overcome crop water stress. Irrigated areas are those areas which are irrigated one or more times during crop growing season.
Rainfed areas have no irrigation whatsoever and are precipitation dependent.
Please collect data on non-cropland areas, especially those near to cropland.
